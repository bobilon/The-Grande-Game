## What This Is

This repository holds two things that belong together, kept in one place so the totality is preserved.

The first is the **public post** — the piece put up on Medium and r/Banksy that puts the Banksy question on the documentary record: the post-Wednesday analysis of the May 20 2026 auction results, and the short NEWSBREAK that follows it. That post is the last I will have to say on the Banksy question under this byline until I publish a separate, fuller work documenting the totality of the experience and what it taught me.

The second is **The Grand Game** — the seven-strand cultural-studies work the post rests on. It integrates art into a synthetic history of roughly the last two hundred and fifty years, with the through-lines kept contained — held to the minimum needed — to inform the post. The same Grand Game is published on Medium. It is preserved here, bound together with the post, so that anyone who reads the public argument can follow it straight down into the documentary structure underneath it without leaving this one place.

The seven strands were not written as reading material for a general audience. They were written as the load-bearing structure under the public argument. I am binding them with the post so the work exists as one self-contained record, for posterity and for the later integrated history to draw on.

I am not big on information capture. This is open. No paywall, no tracking, no ask. If it is useful, take from it; if it is not, leave it.

A note on scope: the documentary apparatus the work rests on — UK Companies House filings, Jersey Financial Services Commission filings, named auction-house catalog records with lot numbers and dates, named books with authors, public-domain literary sources, and dated press articles — is what carries the structural claims. Where a specific claim about a living named individual would require evidence that is not publicly verifiable, the work stays at the structural level and does not name. That wall is held throughout.
